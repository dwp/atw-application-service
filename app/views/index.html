Access to Work: Apply Journey

This page is the repository for the prototypes used by Team Sparrow for the Alpha and Beta testing stages for the Apply Journey for Access to Work.

GitHub

Public-facing code repository can be located on GitHub (opens new tab)

α Accessibility Testing In progress

User Research has strongly indicated that the Live service Team Sparrow inherited does not meet the accessibility standards required by Government and GDS. The prototypes in this section look to test several accessibility hypothesis during the alpha phase.

First round of UR Accessibility Testing scheduled the week commencing 30th August 2021.

1. Accessibility Support page on GOV.UK

GitHub: /1-alpha-accessibility

Research has indicated that the current GOV.UK page for Access to Work does not offer enough signposting and guidance to those with accessibility needs. The purpose of our A/B testing is to test how users would navigate the current service versus a service that includes a new Accessibility Support page.

2. Support based on claimant disability-types

GitHub: /2-alpha-accessibility

Team Sparrow spoke to many users during the discovery phase and it was clear that the level of support currently on offer by GOV and DWP is currently not good enough. For example, 15% of claimants are deaf or hard of hearing and 12% of claimants are blind or have difficulty seeing. This emphasises the importance of developing an accessible-by-default service. The aim of these prototypes is to test how users react to being offered support or a bespoke application journey at the start of their application. Currently we are unsure on how to offer the support, hence a number of prototypes are to be tested and iterated on over time.

2a. How would you prefer to apply?

This prototype directly asks the claimant their preferred application method before they start their application. For example, someone who is dyslexic, may prefer to have a phone call instead of filling out a digital form.

2b. Do you need support completing this application?

The aim of this prototype is to ask the claimant at the start of their application on whether they need support or not. If they require support, we want to test an Access to Work accessibility information page.

2c. British Sign Language (BSL)

A high proportion of claimants speak BSL. What if we identified them at the very start of the application and aided them on a more BSL-bespoke application journey.

3. 'Get help' support column

GitHub: /3-alpha-accessibility

When speaking to users it was clear that the 'Get help' support column on the current Live service does not offer accurate, informative or useful guidance. The aim of these prototypes is to test different types of support content for our users.

4. BSL videos

GitHub: /4-alpha-accessibility

Team Sparrow spoke to several BSL speakers, they all requested BSL videos to be embeded into the service next to each question. The aim of these prototypes is to test different ways of adding a static BSL video (static image - for now!) into the service.

α Eligibility Testing In progress

User Research and an in-depth Design review on the current Live service Team Sparrow inherited highlighted several key concerns:

  • Many potential claimants were not applying for Access to Work because they believed that they were not eligible
  • Many users were making claims despite not being eligible
  • When filling out the application, many users would get rejected half way through their application (having wasted a lot of time filling out details prior to the question that rejected them). This is bad design.
  • A lot of time is currently being wasted by advisors who have to do some back-end investigations into if the claimant is eligible for Access to Work. This indictaed that the current live service is not designed optimally.

The aim of this section of alpha is to uncover the best and simplest ways to determine the eligibility of a claimant

Control Eligibility Prototype

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-control

The following prototype is the control eligibility prototype that contains all of the information currently required by Policy to confirm the eligibility of a claimant. Our overall aim is to iterate on this prototype to make sure it tests well, to optimise it and make it simpler.

Currently the control prototype asks for no personally identifiable information.

New GDS eligibility page

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-gds

During discovery, Team Sparrow has uncovered a lot of information that could help optimise the current eligibility page on GOV.UK. We would like to A/B test the currently live screen against a newer version.

1. Working out eligibility for every job-type

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-1

It has become increasingly apparent that case managers struggle with the 'niche' employment types. For example there are complexities if someone is applying for Access to Work via the Department for Education supported internship, and even more complexities if they are applying for this internship from Wales.

Case managers have stated that over time a lot of different employment-types have been stappled onto Access to Work. If a 'niche' employment type emerges in an application it can be very confusing, complex and time consuming for both the applicant and advisor.

Our hypothesis is that: we believe that identifying and separating out unique job types at the start of an application will result in a better application experience and work out if someone is genuinely eligible.

1a. Completely separating out some Access to Work services

The purpose of this prototype is to alter the GDS GOV.UK homepage to separate out some services into a new sub-services with bespoke and accurate application and eligibility content for the user and advisor.

1b. Optimising the 'What is your employment status' screen

By utilising good content and signposting, this prototype tries to incorporate all employment-types during the eligibility stage.

2. Personal Information & Eligibility

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-2

The control prototype asks all the questions that are required by Policy to determine if a claimant is eligible for Access to Work. The control prototype does not ask for any personally identifiable information.

We believe that by asking for some personally identifiable information during the eligibility stage, we can ask fewer questions and make the application process simpler. For example if we ask for their National Insurance number we can route it to a back-end API to instantly check if they are eligible from an age, citizenship and benefit-status perspective.

Our concern is that claimants may not want to reveal so much identifiable information during the eligibility stage and there may be some GDPR and security considerations.

2a. Name, date of birth and address

This prototype replicates the control prototype, but asks for the name, date of birth and address of the claimant. It is believed that this information would not need to be re-asked during the Apply stage.

2b. National Insurance number

This prototype replicates the control prototype, but requests the National Insurance number of the claimant to do some back-end eligibility checks.

2c. National Insurance number - Automatically Ineligibile

This prototype replicates prototype 2b but aims to show an ineligible screen to the user to gauge how they would interpret the content

3. Eligibility Structure

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-3

Different services throughout Government do eligibility checks differently. We are interested in whether eligibility should be highlighted to the claimants once they have done their eligibility check. Or alternatively, should eligibility seamlessly flow into apply.

3a. "Based on your answers you may be eligible"

This prototype takes inspiration from the Apply for Blue Badge service which contains a "you may be eligible" holding page, with information on what you need to do for the "start application" (apply) stage.

It is also hypothesized that if we make the claimant aware that they "passed eligibility", they will be understanding/forgiving if they get asked a similar question during apply e.g. if we ask the claimant during eligibility-checking if they are "16 or over" (to avoid asking for personal information), they will be forgiving when asked for their date of birth during apply.

3b. Unnoticeable transition from eligibility to apply

This prototype aims for a subtle transition between eligibility and apply, so it appears as one application.

4. Ineligibility Screens

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-4

We believe that claimants who are ineligible will need screens with information into why they are ineligible. We are keen to test the structure, layout and format of these screens.
