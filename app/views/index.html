α Accessibility testing Completed

User Research (UR) has strongly indicated that the live service Team Sparrow inherited does not meet the accessibility standards required by Government and Government Digital Service (GDS). The prototypes in this section aim to test several accessibility hypotheses during the alpha phase.

Round 1 UR Testing

2. Support based on claimant disability types

GitHub: /2-alpha-accessibility

Team Sparrow spoke to many users during the discovery phase and it was clear that the level of support currently on offer by GOV and DWP is currently not good enough. For example, 15% of claimants are deaf or hard of hearing and 12% of claimants are blind or have difficulty seeing. This emphasises the importance of developing an accessible-by-default service. The aim of these prototypes is to test how users react to being offered support or a bespoke application journey at the start of their application. Currently we are unsure on how to offer the support, hence a number of prototypes are to be tested and iterated on over time.

2a. How would you prefer to apply?

This prototype directly asks the claimant their preferred application method before they start their application. For example, someone who is dyslexic, may prefer to have a phone call instead of filling out a digital form.

2b. Do you need support completing this application?

The aim of this prototype is to ask the claimant at the start of their application on whether they need support or not. If they require support, we want to test an Access to Work accessibility information page.

2c. British Sign Language (BSL)

A high proportion of claimants speak BSL. What if we identified them at the very start of the application and aided them on a more BSL-bespoke application journey.

α Eligibility testing completed

UR and a design review on the current Live service Team Sparrow inherited highlighted several key concerns:

  • many potential claimants were not applying for Access to Work because they believed that they were not eligible
  • many users were making claims despite not being eligible
  • when filling out the application, many users would get rejected halfway through their application (having wasted a lot of time filling out details prior to the question that rejected them)
  • a lot of time is currently being wasted by advisors who have to do some back-end investigations into if the claimant is eligible for Access to Work

The aim of this section of alpha is to uncover the best and simplest ways to determine the eligibility of a claimant.

Round 1 UR Testing

2. Personal Information & Eligibility

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-2

The control prototype asks all the questions that are required by Policy to determine if a claimant is eligible for Access to Work. The control prototype does not ask for any personally identifiable information.

We believe that by asking for some personally identifiable information during the eligibility stage, we can ask fewer questions and make the application process simpler. For example if we ask for their National Insurance number we can route it to a back-end API to instantly check if they are eligible from an age, citizenship and benefit-status perspective.

Our concern is that claimants may not want to reveal so much identifiable information during the eligibility stage and there may be some GDPR and security considerations.

2a. Name, date of birth and address

This prototype replicates the control prototype, but asks for the name, date of birth and address of the claimant. It is believed that this information would not need to be re-asked during the Apply stage.

2b. Eligibility reiterated at start - Checkbox

We believe that by reiterating the eligibility criteria before a user applies, we will filter out ineligible people who may not have read the criteria on GOV.UK.

2c. National Insurance number

This prototype replicates the control prototype, but requests the National Insurance number of the claimant to do some back-end eligibility checks.

2d. National Insurance number - Automatically Ineligibile

This prototype replicates prototype 2c but aims to show an ineligible screen to the user to gauge how they would interpret the content.

3. Eligibility Structure

GitHub: /alpha-eligibility-3

Different services throughout Government do eligibility checks differently. We are interested in whether eligibility should be highlighted to the claimants once they have done their eligibility check. Or alternatively, should eligibility seamlessly flow into apply.

Landing page for UR testing.

3a. "Based on your answers you may be eligible"

This prototype takes inspiration from the Apply for Blue Badge service which contains a "you may be eligible" holding page, with information on what you need to do for the "start application" (apply) stage.

It is also hypothesized that if we make the claimant aware that they "passed eligibility", they will be understanding/forgiving if they get asked a similar question during apply e.g. if we ask the claimant during eligibility-checking if they are "16 or over" (to avoid asking for personal information), they will be forgiving when asked for their date of birth during apply.

3b. Eligibility without 'Access to Work helpline' content

This prototype replicates 3a but omits the 'Access to Work helpline' content.

3c. Unnoticeable transition from eligibility to apply

This prototype aims for a subtle transition between eligibility and apply, so it appears as one application.

α Apply journey testing in progress

Apply Journey Prototypes

Since the alpha accessibility and eligibility prototypes have tested very well, we are comfortable to proceed with the apply journey. Having engaged with policy and key DWP stakeholders, we have a rough remit to start exploring how we design the apply service. The aim of these prototypes are to test a series of designs that work well for the claimants, advisors and workplace assessors.

The following designs are based on the scenario that the user knows what support they need from Access to Work. The current thinking is that we will allow someone to book a workplace assessment if they don’t what support they need.

1a. Apply Journey

GitHub: /alpha-apply-1a

This prototype is an improved end-to-end application process that incorporates the previous accessibility and eligibility prototypes.

! Warning As 1b, the task list, was our riskiest assumption, we began prototyping and testing this first. It has tested so well that currently we do not see the need to prototype an end-to-end journey without the task list component. As 1a would be similar to the existing application journey, we have decided to put this idea on hold until we have decided whether to move forward with the task list component.

1b. Apply Journey - Task List

GitHub: /alpha-apply-1b

This prototype is an end-to-end application process that makes use of the task list pattern for the user to complete their application once they have passed eligibility. This design asks about the user’s needs for every element of Access to Work and is quite lengthy, hence the use of the task list pattern.

This design currently assumes that save and return functionality would be possible.

1c. Apply Journey - Task List Iteration

Start: 2nd November 2021

GitHub: /alpha-apply-1c

Prototype 1c includes content and design iterations based on user feedback, and the introduction of some other ideas we wanted to test. These include:

  • Addition of ‘Save progress’ screens to see users’ reactions
  • Removal of quotes from all journeys except ‘Travelling to and from work’ and ‘Support workers’
  • Addition of patterns to allow users to add conditions and specialist equipment one-by-one
  • Moving the question ‘Do you know what support you need?’ into each element, instead of at the start of the journey

1d. Apply Journey - Iteration

Start: 15th November 2021

GitHub: /alpha-apply-1d

Prototype 1d takes into account content and design iterations based on user feedback and content crits. These include:

  • Development of the ‘Travelling to and from work’ journey
  • Moving the declaration to its own screen so that the user doesn’t miss it before submitting
  • Introduction of ‘Optional’ statuses on the task list menu
  • Removal of the ‘Save progress’ screens, as we may not be able to offer this from a technical standpoint so we’re removing it from prototypes for now
  • Reverting the benefits eligibility question to reflect what was included in some of the early eligibility prototypes (for example, in the control prototype), based on case manager feedback
  • Other content and design tweaks based on testing feedback

1e. Apply Journey - Iteration

Start: 1st December 2021

GitHub: /alpha-apply-1e

Prototype 1e will encompass feedback from internal and external users that we tested with during the week commencing 22 November 2021. These iterations will include:

  • Update GOV.UK guide to reflect the new content that is live
  • Accessibility iterations to reduce focus on BSL
  • More clarity around workplace contact
  • Tweak to explanations of heading to ‘Support’ title on task list
  • Addition of Access to Work helpline information throughout the application form
  • Other content and design tweaks based on user feedback

1f. Apply Journey - Iteration

Start: 9th December 2021

GitHub: /alpha-apply-1f

Prototype 1f includes many technical and content tweaks, in order to get the journey into an ‘MVP’ state before the Christmas break 2021. These changes include:

  • Addition of tagging functionality to the task list, to accurately reflect the progress that a user makes through each section of the application
  • Improvements to ‘Check your answers’ functionality and addition of a final ‘Check your answers’ before submission
  • Addition of optional equality questions before submission
  • Addition of a ‘length of journey’ question within the ‘Travelling to and from work’ section, if a user currently uses public transport to get to work
  • Tweaks to the accessibility questions, to remove British Sign Language (BSL) video option
  • Changes to the contact preferences screens, as we still need to collect email and telephone number for applicants, regardless of their contact preference
  • Further content tweaks based on crit feedback
